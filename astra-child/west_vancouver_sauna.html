The Coastal Challenge: Engineering for High Gradient & Marine Exposure

West Vancouver's iconic rocky coastlines demand rigorous structural engineering. Building a panoramic glass thermal structure on steep-gradient bedrock requires more than aesthetic execution. It requires solving complex geotechnical stability, high wind shear, salt-spray corrosion, and extreme temperature differential pressures.

At Keystone Possibilities, our luxury outdoor saunas are designed and executed under the direct oversight of Wayne Stevenson (BC Builder License #52603), ensuring absolute structural integrity from foundation pinning to glass installation.

Geotechnical and Glazing Specifications

1. Geotechnical Foundation & Bedrock Pinning

We do not rely on standard footings. Our foundations are anchored directly into solid coastal granite. We drill up to 3 meters into the bedrock, installing post-tensioned, epoxy-grouted steel rock anchors. This custom pinning system supports a cantilevered engineered concrete platform capable of handling immense shear and gravity loads without risk of slip or subsidence.

2. High-Impact, Low-E Laminated Architectural Glass

To deliver an unobstructed panoramic view of the Pacific while resisting extreme thermal deltas, we utilize custom triple-glazed, structural low-E laminated glass. The structural glazing is engineered to withstand coastal storm wind loads up to 3.5 kPa. A specialized, flexible silicone structural glaze handles the expansion and contraction associated with a 90°C (194°F) internal heat cycle against cold marine winds.

3. Marine-Grade Corrosion Mitigation (Grade 316 Stainless Steel)

Saltwater air destroys standard structural steel and zinc-coated hardware. Suna Spa structures utilize structural framing elements in grade 316 stainless steel, heavy-duty marine-hardened brackets, and advanced protective coatings. All wood components are selected from premium, tight-grain Western Red Cedar to resist rot and insects in a damp coastal environment.
